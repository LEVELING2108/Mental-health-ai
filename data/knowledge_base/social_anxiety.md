# Understanding Social Anxiety
Social anxiety is more than just shyness; it is an intense fear of being watched and judged by others in social or performance situations.

### Coping Strategies:
1. **Focus Outward:** When in a social situation, try to focus on the person you are talking to or your surroundings, rather than your internal sensations or self-critical thoughts.
2. **Small Steps (Exposure):** Don't avoid social situations entirely. Start with low-pressure environments and gradually work your way up to more challenging ones.
3. **Challenge "Mind Reading":** We often assume we know what others are thinking (usually something negative). Remind yourself that you cannot read minds and that most people are focused on themselves, not you.
4. **Prepare, Don't Rehearse:** It's okay to think of a few conversation starters, but avoid over-rehearsing every word, as this can increase pressure.
5. **The 3-3-3 Rule:** When feeling anxious, name 3 things you see, 3 sounds you hear, and move 3 parts of your body. This helps ground you in the present.
