# Managing Panic Attacks
A panic attack is a sudden episode of intense fear that triggers severe physical reactions when there is no real danger or apparent cause.

### Immediate Actions:
1. **Recognize the Attack:** Remind yourself: "This is a panic attack. It is temporary, and I am safe. I am not in physical danger."
2. **Deep, Slow Breathing:** Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 2, and exhale for 6. Focus entirely on the sensation of air entering and leaving your lungs.
3. **Find a Focal Point:** Pick one object in your sight and notice everything about it—its color, shape, texture, and how the light hits it.
4. **Use Lavender or Peppermint:** Strong, pleasant scents can help ground you and provide a different sensory focus.
5. **Muscle Relaxation:** Tense and release your shoulders. Often, we carry immense tension there during an attack without realizing it.

*Important: If you are experiencing chest pain or shortness of breath for the first time, it is important to seek medical evaluation to rule out other conditions.*
